Research Areas

Protein Tyrosine Kinase Regulation

We investigate the regulation of protein tyrosine kinase activity in signal transduction pathways using a combination of NMR spectroscopy and molecular dynamics simulations. Our work focuses on understanding allosteric mechanisms that control kinase activation and the conformational changes that occur during catalysis.

Current Projects:

  • Syk kinase activation mechanisms
  • Allosteric regulation in receptor tyrosine kinases
  • Drug discovery targeting kinase conformational states

Protein Thermodynamics

Our lab estimates thermodynamic properties of protein systems to understand stability, folding mechanisms, and intermolecular interactions. We combine experimental calorimetry with computational approaches to characterize the energetics of protein-protein and protein-ligand interactions.

Research Methods:

  • Isothermal titration calorimetry (ITC)
  • Free energy perturbation calculations
  • Temperature-dependent NMR studies
  • Statistical thermodynamics modeling

Prion Dynamics

We explore the mechanisms of prion dynamics and misfolding processes that lead to neurodegenerative diseases using computational approaches. Our work aims to understand how protein conformational changes contribute to prion propagation and infectivity.

Focus Areas:

  • Prion protein conformational transitions
  • Aggregation kinetics and mechanisms
  • Computational models of misfolding
  • Structure-function relationships in prion diseases

NMR Methodology Development

We develop new NMR spectroscopic methods for studying protein dynamics and interactions. Our methodological advances enable better characterization of protein flexibility, binding kinetics, and conformational exchange processes.

Technical Innovations:

  • Relaxation dispersion experiments
  • Chemical shift perturbation analysis
  • Paramagnetic relaxation enhancement
  • Integration with molecular dynamics

Our Research Philosophy

The Post Lab is committed to advancing our understanding of protein function through the integration of experimental and computational approaches. We believe that protein dynamics are central to biological function, and our research aims to bridge the gap between atomic-level structure and physiological activity.

Our interdisciplinary approach combines cutting-edge NMR spectroscopy with state-of-the-art molecular simulations to provide comprehensive insights into protein behavior. We are passionate about developing new methodologies that can reveal previously hidden aspects of protein dynamics and their relationship to disease.
